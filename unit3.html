Unit III:

The Chemistry of Life: Biomolecules Outlook

Overview

The human body is made up of different organic and inorganic molecules that help in maintaining the different cellular activities essential for living. Almost two-thirds of the body is made up of water; hence, water plays a significant role in cells. Most chemical reactions in cells involve different elements and compounds that vary in function. Having a correct dose of these essential molecules can lead to life diversity.

This unit will enrich your understanding on the biological compounds found in the body. It includes the role of simple elements in complex organisms. Different biological processes involving the interaction of different essential elements and various compounds are discussed in this chapter.
